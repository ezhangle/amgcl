Detailed Description

Plain aggregation.

Modification of a greedy aggregation scheme from [7]. Connectivity is defined in a symmetric way, that is, two variables and are considered to be connected to each other if . Variables without neighbours (resulting, e.g., from Dirichlet conditions) are excluded from aggregation process. The aggregation is completed in a single pass over variables: variables adjacent to a new aggregate are temporarily marked as beloning to this aggregate. Later they may be claimed by other aggregates; if nobody claims them, then they just stay in their initial aggregate.
